Visa Officer Psychology & Tips

How to behave in front of the window.

The 10-Second Rule

The VO (Visa Officer) does 100+ interviews a day. They are exhausted. They make up their mind in the first 10 seconds. Never start an answer with background fluff. Give the punchline immediately (The Hook), then explain.

Handling Interruptions

Officers WILL interrupt you mid-sentence. This is a stress test. If they interrupt, stop talking immediately. Do not try to finish your sentence. Listen to the new question and pivot seamlessly.

Document Discipline

Never shove documents through the window unprompted. Wait for them to ask. Say: "My father is funding me with an 85 Lakh FD. I have the bank statement here if you'd like to see it." Let them invite it.

Body Language

Stand straight. Keep your hands visible on the counter, not in your pockets. Look the officer in the eye, not at the keyboard or your documents. You are a scientist discussing your future, act like one.
